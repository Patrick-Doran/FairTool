Stage 1 Definition Notes
Identify the Asset at Risk Anything that may be affected in a manner whereby its value is diminished or the act introduces liability.
Identify the Threat Community A threat agent is anything capable of acting against an asset in a manner that can result in harm. The threat community is a subset of the overall threat agent population that shares key characteristics.
Identify the Loss Event Occurs when a threat agent's action (threat event) is successful in negatively affecting an asset.
Stage 2 Definition Notes
Estimate the Threat Capability The probable level of force that a threat agent is capable of applying against an asset. Utilize OWASP classifications
Determine Contact Frequency The probable frequency, within a given timeframe, that a threat agent will come into contact with an asset. Impacted by CIS Controls Assessment (Avoindance Controls)
Determine Probability of Action The probability that a threat agent will act against an asset once contact occurs. Impacted by CIS Controls Assessment (Deterrence Controls)
Determine Resistance Strength (Difficulty) The strength of a control as compared to a standard measure of force. Also labeled 'Difficulty.' Impacted by CIS Controls Assessment (Difficulty Controls)
Derive Threat Event Frequency The probable frequency, within a given timeframe, that a threat agent will act against an asset. Calculated from Contact Frequency and Probability of Action
Derive Vulnerability The probability that an asset will be unable to resist the actions of a threat agent. Calculated from Threat Capability and Resistance Strength
Derive Primary Loss Event Frequency The probable frequency, within a given timeframe, that a threat agent will inflict harm upon an asset. Calculated from Threat Event Frequency and Vulnerability
Stage 3 Definition Notes
Estimate Primary Loss Magnitude Losses resulting from decreased productivity, response effore, and/or replacement of asset at risk Impacted by CIS Controls Assessment (Responsive Controls)
Estimate Secondary Loss Probability The probability that a secondary loss event may occur.
Derive Secondary Loss Event Frequency The probable frequency, within a given timeframe, that a secondary loss event will occur.
Estimate Secondary Loss Magnitude Losses resulting from fines and judgement; competitive advantage; and/or requtational damage
Stage 4 Definition
Derive Primary Risk Calculated based on Loss Event Frequency and Primary Loss Magnitude
Derive Secondary Risk Calculated based on Secondary Loss Event Frequency and Secondary Loss Magnitude
Derive Overall Risk Calculated based on Primary Risk and Secondary Risk
